PROJECT OVERVIEW

Business Health (Overview)
The dashboard establishes a strong baseline with $52M in total revenue and a healthy $20.6 average income per KM. It highlights a stable business model where monthly revenue consistently hovers around the $4.2M–$4.5M range, supported by a peak booking volume of nearly 13,000 rides in January.

2. Financial Ecosystem (Revenue)
The analysis reveals a heavy shift toward digital transformation. With 45% of transactions via UPI and nearly 67% total digital adoption (UPI + Wallet + Cards), the business benefits from reduced cash-handling friction. Revenue is highly resilient, showing minimal seasonality and consistent monthly performance.

3. Marketplace Sentiment (Rating)
A comparative study of the two-sided marketplace shows high customer satisfaction (4.7/5.0) but identifies a slight lag in driver satisfaction (4.3/5.0). The Go Sedan tier emerges as the "Gold Standard," leading in satisfaction scores for both user groups, while the Uber XL segment presents the most room for quality improvement.

4. Operational Friction (Cancellation)
This is the most critical area for strategic intervention. The dashboard identifies a 38% cancellation rate (57K failed bookings). Crucially, it distinguishes between user error and operational failure, noting that 44% of customer cancellations are due to driver-related issues (driver not moving or asking to cancel), representing a significant "revenue leak."

5. Fleet Performance (Vehicle)
The final pillar breaks down the fleet into seven distinct tiers. It identifies Auto ($12.88M) and Go Mini ($10.34M) as the primary revenue engines. The data reveals a standardized pricing strategy of $20.63/KM across all vehicle types, suggesting that growth is driven by volume and distance rather than premium pricing.

Cancellation & Lost Opportunity Analysis
The Cancellation Dashboard investigates why rides fail to complete. High cancellation rates directly impact revenue and user trust, making this the primary focus for operational improvements.

Core Efficiency Metrics
Total Booking Requests: 150K

Successful Bookings: 93K

Cancelled Bookings: 57K

Overall Cancellation Rate: 38%

Root Cause Analysis
1. Customer Cancellations (By Reason)
The data shows a relatively even split among the top four reasons for customer-led cancellations (~22% each):

Operational Friction: "Driver not moving towards pickup" and "Driver asked to cancel" account for a combined 44% of customer cancellations, indicating a need for better driver accountability.

User Error: "Wrong Address" and "Change of plans" represent the remaining bulk.

Vehicle Quality: A smaller but notable 11% cancelled because the "AC was not working."

2. Driver Cancellations (By Reason)
Driver cancellations are evenly distributed at approximately 25% per major category:

Safety & Health: "The customer was coughing/sick" and "More than permitted people" are significant drivers of cancellations.

Technical/Personal: "Personal & Car related issues" account for 24.9% of driver-led drops.

Revenue Impact: With a 38% cancellation rate, the business has a massive opportunity to recover "lost revenue" by addressing the 44% of cancellations caused by driver behavior (asking to cancel or not moving).

Quality Control: The 11% "AC not working" cancellation rate suggests that a small investment in vehicle maintenance checks could improve the completion rate.

Volume vs. Value: The difference in total booking value is driven entirely by Total Ride Distance (Volume) rather than price-per-km variance.

Go Sedan holds the highest ratings from both customers (4.78) and drivers (4.32), indicating it is the most well-balanced service tier in terms of satisfaction and operational harmony.
